What are Sanctions?
Sanctions are tools of foreign policies applied by governments, groups of countries, or international organizations on another country, entity or individual who pose a threat to international peace and security.
 
Types of Sanctions
Economic Sanctions
To restrict or ban financial transactions and bilateral trade/commerce.
Diplomatic Sanctions
To limit or cancel high-level government visits or expel or withdraw diplomatic missions or staff.
Military Sanctions
To cut off supplies of weapons, military equipment or dual-use items.
Sport Sanctions
To prevent a country, its citizens and its teams from competing in international sporting events like the World Cup and Olympics.
Who can be sanctioned?
Countries, Individuals, Entities, Vessels, Aircrafts.

Sanctions can be imposed when any of the following occurs:
  • A violation of international law.
  • There is a threat to international peace and security.
  • Cases of human rights abuses. 
  • Financing the proliferation of weapons of mass destruction. 
  • Financing terrorism-related activities.

Where are the lists of sanctioned entities uploaded?

Most countries have local sanctions lists and bodies that issue and enforce them. Internationally, the following bodies issue and enforce varying sanctions and the sanctions lists can be obtained from their websites:
  • United Nations (UN);
  • European Union (EU).

Popular sanction bodies and their countries are:
USA - Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC);
UK - His Majesty’s Treasury (HMT);
Canada - Global Affairs Canada (GAC);
Nigeria - Nigeria Sanctions Committee (NIGSAC)
